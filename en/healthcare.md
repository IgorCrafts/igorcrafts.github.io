# Healthcare coordination is broken


<div class="landing-subintro" markdown="1">
Care relies on fragile workflows and workarounds. <br/>When they fail, patients pay the price.
</div>

{% comment %}

This piece starts from a simple observation: as soon as you get close to real patient journeys, you realize how much of healthcare runs on fragile, ad‑hoc workflows.

I’m still writing the full version of this article.  
For now, think of it as a sketchpad for:

- patterns I’ve seen repeatedly across services and countries,  
- where coordination breaks for patients, families, and clinicians,  
- and why “workflow debt” is just as real as technical debt.

It made me extremely impatient with inefficiency and deeply respectful of frontline healthcare workers who face constant friction.

The published version will be opinionated, concrete, and grounded in real stories (anonymized).
